  • Street Furniture for Two Italian Towns

Of all the architectural objects you might expect to have some liveliness to them, traffic bollards don't tend to make it near the top of the list, but while studying abroad in Italy, I couldn’t help but see these gumdrop-shaped concrete bollards as little creatures. They were always arranged in peculiar ways, as if they were living their little bollard lives and when I turned to look at them, they froze in place.

Their design, simultaneously cute and utilitarian, has inspired the design for different pieces of street furniture for two towns in rural Italy. Together they form a big happy family.

The smaller members of this family can be placed all over the towns, responding to spontaneous town activities.

Their larger relatives are idiosyncratic creatures that respond to town landmarks: a cemetery, two churches, and an elementary school.
